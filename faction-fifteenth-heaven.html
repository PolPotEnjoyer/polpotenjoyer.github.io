Cult of the Fifteenth Heaven

The Cult of the Fifteenth Heaven is a relatively new religious movement that sprang up during the early settlement of Quantrill's Plain. The name refers to the cult's belief in fifteen heavens: one each for the fourteen Craters - the Lower Heavens, and a Heaven Ascendant which can only be reached by those who have attained enlightenment. Characterized by a distrust for outsiders and the traditional social systems and hierarchies of the Craters, Fifteenth Heaveners are most often found in a scattered network of hidden monasteries across the Plain, though they also dominate the politics of Zapel's Town, which they both founded and have their own district in, making up around half the population. They also hold a small quarter in Rondezvous and a few individuals are likely to be found somewhere in Quantrill City.

History

The origins of the Fifteenth Heavener Cult, including the exact date of their foundation, are shrouded in mystery. According to themselves - the only real source available - their founder was a mysterious Hamen woman known as Zapel the Illuminator. Following the story, Zapel was a blind widow of humble means who regained her sight through the use of Sansite, when all other means had failed. This minor miracle set her on a long quest for the truth, and the full potential of Sansite to bring about more and greater miracles, a journey towards enlightenment and ascension. When the First Sansite Rush began, Zapel was one of the first generation of permanent settlers, quickly gathering a following among prospectors both hopeful and bankrupt. Together they founded a then-isolated community in the Plain's far Northwest, which later came to be named after her. Zapel disappears from all record after a few years, presumed dead.

As mining operations continued to expand and the Quantrill-Calumet Mining Company became yet more powerful, the cult's isolation was threatened. Prospectors inevitably began to settle around Zapel's Town, and though efforts were made to evict them the outnumbered Fifteenth-Heaveners were eventually forced to allow them to settle in a seperate quarter. This loss of isolation motivated the growth of the cult's diaspora across the Plain, with a small, well-hidden monastery network emerging to echo the Company's stations. Today, the cult of the Fifteenth Heaven forms a major, if somewhat passive, force in Upland Politics and appears to continue to grow, though their secretive nature evades reliable inspection.
